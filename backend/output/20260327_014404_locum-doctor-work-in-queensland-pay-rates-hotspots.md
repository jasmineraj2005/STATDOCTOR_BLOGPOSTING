## What are the key locum hotspots in Queensland?

Queensland's vast landscape is managed by 16 Hospital and Health Services (HHSs), each with its own board, budget, and credentialing process. From Cairns to the Gold Coast, these HHSs operate over 130 public hospitals. The demand for locum doctors is high, with Queensland Health spending A$23.8 billion in 2024-25 to meet healthcare needs, as reported by the [Queensland Audit Office](https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5826T0019/5826t19.pdf).

In Far North Queensland, the Cairns and Hinterland and Torres and Cape HHSs offer some of the highest locum pay rates in Australia, often exceeding A$3,000 per day. These regions cover large areas with remote communities heavily reliant on locum coverage. Townsville and Mackay in North Queensland also maintain consistent demand, particularly in emergency departments and general medicine.

Central and Western Queensland, including areas like Mount Isa and Rockhampton, often see peak rates due to their remote locations. These regions offer a broad clinical scope, with accommodation and flights typically included. In the South East, Brisbane's Metro North and Metro South HHSs provide a high volume of shifts, albeit at lower rates compared to more remote areas.

## How do pay rates for locum doctors in Queensland compare?

Queensland's locum pay rates are competitive, with specific details affecting comparisons. For GP VMOs in rural areas, rates range from A$1,800 to over A$3,000 per day. Emergency department specialists can earn between A$2,500 and A$4,000 per shift, especially in high-demand regions like Mount Isa and Torres and Cape. Hospital RMOs and HMOs typically see rates of A$140 to A$175 per hour, with night shifts offering premiums of A$160+ per hour.

A critical detail is that Queensland's pay rates generally include superannuation, unlike NSW where super is often paid on top. This means a A$150/hr rate in Queensland effectively includes 11.5% super, impacting cash-in-hand comparisons across states.

## What do locum doctors need to work in Queensland?

To work as a locum doctor in Queensland, several requirements must be met. A current and unconditional [AHPRA registration](https://www.ahpra.gov.au) is essential. Doctors must also have an ABN for contractor payment, with guidance available in our [ABN setup guide](https://statdoctor.app/post//post/getting-an-abn-as-a-locum-doctor-a-step-by-step-guide).

Medical indemnity insurance is crucial, and more information can be found in our [insurance and legal requirements guide](https://statdoctor.app/post/essential-insurance-and-legal-considerations-for-locum-doctors). A national police check, a Blue Card for working with children, and HHS-specific credentialing are also required. Vaccination records must be up-to-date, including Hep B, MMR, varicella, dTpa, COVID, influenza, and TB screening.

## What does this mean for locum doctors in Queensland?

Locum doctors in Queensland must navigate a complex landscape of credentialing and agreements. The B45 Locum Health Agreement framework governs placements in the public system, requiring agreements with specific HHSs. Not all agencies have agreements with all 16 HHSs, so it's vital to check before committing.

Interstate doctors should note that holding a contract with Queensland Health's public system precludes simultaneous locum shifts within the state's public network. However, working in the private sector or interstate is allowed. Understanding these nuances is critical for effective locum work in Queensland.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What are the highest paying locum positions in Queensland?**
A: Some of the highest paying positions are in remote areas like Far North Queensland, where locum doctors can earn over A$3,000 per day. Emergency department specialists in regions such as Mount Isa and Torres and Cape also see high rates, often between A$2,500 and A$4,000 per shift.

**Q: How does Queensland's locum pay compare to other states?**
A: Queensland's locum pay is competitive, but it's important to note that rates generally include superannuation. This means a A$150/hr rate in Queensland includes 11.5% super, unlike NSW where super is often paid on top. This affects cash-in-hand comparisons.

**Q: What are the credentialing requirements for locum work in Queensland?**
A: Credentialing is required for each of the 16 Hospital and Health Services (HHSs) in Queensland, as they operate independently. This includes AHPRA registration, a national police check, a Blue Card, and specific HHS credentialing processes.

**Q: Are there seasonal variations in locum demand in Queensland?**
A: Yes, demand spikes during Christmas, Easter, and school holidays due to Queensland's status as a major domestic tourism state. Locum doctors should plan for increased opportunities during these periods.

**Q: What logistical considerations are there for locum work in remote Queensland?**
A: Remote placements often require flights, which are usually booked by the hospital or agency. Minimum blocks are typically five to seven days. The wet season from December to March can cause flight delays and isolation, particularly in northern regions.

**Q: Can locum doctors work in both the public and private sectors in Queensland?**
A: Locum doctors can work in both sectors, but those with a Queensland Health public system contract cannot simultaneously do locum shifts within the state's public network. Private sector work and interstate locum work are permitted.

# How Are Doctors' Strikes in England Affecting Locum Opportunities in Australia and New Zealand?

---

**TL;DR:** The ongoing doctors' strikes in England have led to increased interest in locum opportunities abroad, particularly in Australia and New Zealand. These countries offer competitive remuneration and a better work-life balance, attracting UK-trained doctors. Consequently, the demand for locum doctors in Australia and New Zealand is rising, especially in rural areas.

## What is the current situation with doctors' strikes in England?

The current situation with doctors' strikes in England is marked by significant industrial action led by the British Medical Association (BMA). These strikes have been primarily driven by disputes over pay and working conditions, which have reached a critical point in recent months. The strikes have escalated, with NHS leaders warning of the "maximum harm" they could cause to healthcare services [source](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2026/mar/26/nhs-bosses-say-resident-doctors-strike-maximum-harm).

The strikes have been strategically timed to exert maximum pressure on the healthcare system, with some coinciding with the Easter break, a period already strained by staff shortages and increased patient demand [source](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2026/apr/08/doctors-strike-easter-break-rota-havoc-nhs-england-chief-jim-mackey). The BMA has been vocal about the need for better pay to match the increasing demands placed on doctors. As a result, there have been significant disruptions to services, with many routine appointments and surgeries being postponed.

The impact of these strikes is profound, not only affecting the delivery of healthcare services in England but also prompting many doctors to reconsider their career paths. There is a growing interest in exploring opportunities abroad, particularly in countries like Australia and New Zealand, where the healthcare systems offer competitive salaries and improved working conditions.

## How could these strikes affect locum opportunities in Australia and New Zealand?

The doctors' strikes in England have significantly influenced locum opportunities in Australia and New Zealand by increasing the pool of available and interested candidates. Many UK doctors are seeking alternatives that offer both financial stability and a healthier work-life balance, making locum positions in these countries highly attractive. This trend is underpinned by the comparability of healthcare systems, cultural similarities, and the allure of living in regions known for their quality of life.

Australia has seen a notable rise in the demand for locum doctors, driven by both domestic workforce shortages and the interest from international doctors affected by the strikes. This demand is particularly pronounced in rural and remote areas of Australia, where healthcare services often struggle to maintain adequate staffing levels. The Australian healthcare system, supported by Medicare, provides attractive remuneration packages for locum doctors, which is appealing to those affected by pay disputes in the UK [source](https://www.health.gov.au/resources/publications/medicare-benefits-schedule-review).

Similarly, New Zealand offers competitive locum opportunities, especially in rural areas where healthcare needs are critical. The smaller scale of New Zealand's healthcare system allows for a more personal work environment, which many doctors find rewarding. Additionally, New Zealand's efforts to attract international medical practitioners have been bolstered by the current situation in the UK, positioning it as a viable option for those looking to practice abroad.

## Are there specific specialties in demand due to these strikes?

Yes, the strikes have spurred demand in specific medical specialties, particularly those that are already experiencing shortages in both Australia and New Zealand. General practitioners, emergency medicine specialists, and psychiatrists are among the most sought-after due to the ongoing shortages and the critical roles they play in healthcare delivery.

General practitioners are in high demand across rural and remote areas in both countries. These areas often face significant challenges in recruiting permanent staff, making locum positions an attractive solution to fill the gaps. The increased interest from UK doctors in these roles is a welcome development, providing much-needed relief to regions that have historically struggled with inadequate staffing levels.

Emergency medicine specialists are also highly sought after, given the essential nature of their work. The high-pressure environment of emergency departments, combined with the fluctuating patient volumes, often necessitates additional staffing that locum doctors can provide. The influx of UK-trained doctors, many of whom are accustomed to the fast-paced NHS environment, can offer immediate and effective relief to these departments.

Psychiatry is another specialty experiencing heightened demand, reflecting global trends in mental health needs. The strikes have highlighted the importance of mental health services, and both Australia and New Zealand are actively seeking professionals who can contribute to this critical area of healthcare. This demand aligns well with the skills of many UK-trained psychiatrists looking for new opportunities abroad.

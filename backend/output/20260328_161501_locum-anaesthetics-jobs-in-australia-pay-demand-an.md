# Locum Anaesthetics Jobs in Australia: Pay, Demand, and What You Need

## Why is demand for locum anaesthetists so high?

Anaesthetics remains the highest-paying locum specialty in Australia due to several key factors. Operating theatre shortages, an ageing population, and chronic staffing gaps mean that anaesthetists are in demand across all states. Hospitals without anaesthetists face idle theatres, cancelled surgeries, and lost revenue, which is why locum anaesthetists command premium rates.

The demand is further driven by an ageing population requiring more surgeries, rural hospitals struggling to recruit permanent staff, and the lengthy FANZCA training pipeline. According to the [ANZCA Anaesthesia Training Program Handbook](https://www.anzca.edu.au/), this training involves 260 weeks of supervised clinical placements. Additionally, private hospitals rely heavily on anaesthetists, making locum cover essential when permanent staff are unavailable.

The Rural Locum Assistance Program (Rural LAP) actively recruits locum anaesthetists for rural placements, offering attractive packages including travel allowances and accommodation.

## What do locum anaesthetists earn?

Current data from Access Anaesthesia and CC Medical indicates that locum anaesthetists earn between A$2,000 and A$4,000 per day. Rates vary based on location and urgency, with rural and remote areas like Western Australia, Northern Territory, and Queensland offering the highest pay. On-call and after-hours work can further increase earnings.

Private practice anaesthetists typically bill A$2,500 to A$5,000 per half-day session, equating to A$500 to A$1,000 per hour. In comparison, permanent public hospital consultants earn approximately A$300,000 to A$450,000 annually, including on-call duties.

## Who can work as a locum anaesthetist?

There are two main pathways for working as a locum anaesthetist in Australia: as a Specialist Anaesthetist (FANZCA) or as a GP Anaesthetist. 

Specialist Anaesthetists with FANZCA and AHPRA specialist registration can access a full range of locum opportunities across public, private, metro, and rural settings. They are highly sought after due to their ability to provide unsupervised anaesthesia across various procedures.

GP Anaesthetists, who hold FRACGP or FACRRM and have completed additional JCCA-endorsed training, are common in rural areas where specialist anaesthetists are scarce. They typically work under specific protocols for a defined scope of procedures.

Both categories require unrestricted AHPRA registration, an ABN, medical indemnity insurance, and compliance with CPD requirements.

## Where are the best locum anaesthetics opportunities?

### Rural and Remote Australia

The highest locum anaesthetics rates are found in rural and remote areas like Northern Territory, rural Western Australia, Far North Queensland, Tasmania, and outback South Australia. These regions offer comprehensive packages including flights, accommodation, and vehicles due to their distance from specialist supply.

### Regional Hospitals

Mid-sized regional hospitals in locations such as Dubbo, Wagga Wagga, Ballarat, Bendigo, Bundaberg, Mackay, and Mount Gambier offer a balance of clinical variety, reasonable rates, and access to town amenities. These facilities often require locum cover when permanent anaesthetists are on leave.

### Private Hospitals

Private hospitals in metro and regional areas hire locum anaesthetists to cover surgical lists and day surgeries. While rates are generally lower than remote public hospital work, conditions are more predictable with modern facilities and defined schedules.

> [KEY TAKEAWAY] Rural and remote placements offer the highest financial return for locum anaesthetists.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What qualifications are needed to work as a locum anaesthetist in Australia?**
A: Locum anaesthetists must have either FANZCA or GP anaesthetist qualifications, along with AHPRA registration. Additional requirements include an ABN, medical indemnity insurance, CPD compliance, and standard credentialing documents.

**Q: How do locum anaesthetists manage their own taxes and insurance?**
A: Locum anaesthetists, often working as independent contractors, need to manage their own taxes and insurance. This includes obtaining an ABN, securing appropriate medical indemnity insurance, and maintaining CPD compliance.

**Q: What is the typical day rate for locum anaesthetists in metro areas?**
A: In metro areas, locum anaesthetics rates typically range from A$2,000 to A$2,500 per day. These rates reflect the lower demand and higher availability of anaesthetists compared to rural and remote areas.

**Q: How does the Rural LAP support locum anaesthetists?**
A: The Rural Locum Assistance Program (Rural LAP) supports locum anaesthetists by offering hourly wages, travel allowances, and covering flights and accommodation for placements in rural and remote areas.

**Q: Are there lifestyle considerations for locum anaesthetists?**
A: Yes, locum anaesthetists often face significant clinical responsibility and may be on call frequently. Rural placements can mean time away from family and support networks, but offer substantial financial and professional rewards.

**Q: Can GP anaesthetists work independently in rural hospitals?**
A: GP anaesthetists typically work under agreed protocols for a defined scope of procedures in rural hospitals. They must have JCCA-endorsed training and CPD participation to ensure safe practice.

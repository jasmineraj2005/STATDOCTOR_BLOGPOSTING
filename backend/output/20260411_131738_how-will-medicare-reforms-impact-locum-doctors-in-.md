# How Will Medicare Reforms Impact Locum Doctors in Australia?
*[15] min read  |  Industry News  |  April 2026*

---

**TL;DR:** Medicare reforms in Australia are expected to change the landscape for locum doctors by altering billing practices and shifting opportunities towards community health settings. These reforms may impact locum doctor income and administrative tasks, while also influencing the demand for locum services in both urban and rural areas.

---

## What are the proposed Medicare reforms?

The proposed Medicare reforms in Australia focus on enhancing efficiency, reducing costs, and prioritising primary care and preventive health. These changes aim to streamline healthcare delivery by updating the Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) and implementing digital health initiatives. The reforms could lead to adjustments in how services are delivered and funded, potentially impacting various aspects of the healthcare system, including the role of locum doctors [source](https://www.ama.com.au/articles/future-australias-health-system-medicare-reforms).

## How could these changes affect locum doctor income?

Locum doctor income may be affected by changes in billing practices under the new Medicare reforms. As the MBS undergoes adjustments, the types of services locum doctors provide and the corresponding fees might change. This could lead to variations in income, as locum doctors may need to adapt their service offerings to align with the revised schedule. Additionally, the administrative workload could increase with new billing procedures, impacting the time available for patient care [source](https://www.health.gov.au/resources/publications/medicare-benefits-schedule-review).

## What should locum doctors in Australia expect?

Locum doctors in Australia should prepare for a shift in opportunities from hospitals to community health settings as the government emphasises primary care and preventive health. This shift may require locum doctors to familiarise themselves with new practice environments and adapt to different patient care models. Furthermore, the introduction of digital health initiatives, such as telehealth services, may necessitate upskilling in technology to remain competitive [source](https://www.aihw.gov.au/reports/workforce/health-workforce-dynamics).

## What does this mean for locum doctors in New South Wales?

For locum doctors in New South Wales, Medicare reforms could result in both challenges and opportunities. With changes in funding allocations, demand for locum services in rural and remote areas may fluctuate, either increasing or decreasing based on specific community needs. Additionally, smaller practices facing financial viability issues might rely more on locum doctors to cover staffing shortages. Locum doctors in New South Wales should stay informed about state-specific initiatives and adapt their services accordingly [source](https://www.health.vic.gov.au/initiatives/locum-support).

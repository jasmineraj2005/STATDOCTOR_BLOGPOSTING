## 📈 What are the current GP shortages in Australia?

Australia is currently experiencing a significant shortage of General Practitioners, a trend that has been intensifying over recent years. This shortage is most acute in rural and remote areas, where healthcare delivery is being severely impacted. The Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW) reports that the number of full-time equivalent GPs per 100,000 population has decreased by 3% over the past five years. This decline highlights the growing challenge of maintaining adequate healthcare services across the country [source](https://www.aihw.gov.au/reports/workforce/health-workforce).

The shortages are driven by several factors, including an aging GP workforce and insufficient numbers of new graduates entering general practice. The Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) has indicated that many older GPs are retiring, further exacerbating the shortage. Additionally, the financial viability of general practice is being strained as Medicare rebates have not kept pace with inflation, discouraging many from pursuing a career in this critical field [source](https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics).

Efforts to mitigate these shortages, such as recruiting international medical graduates, face regulatory hurdles. The Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) has stringent registration requirements that can delay the integration of these professionals into the workforce. As a result, the healthcare system is increasingly reliant on locum doctors to fill these gaps.

## 💰 How do GP shortages influence locum pay rates?

The shortage of GPs in Australia has a direct impact on locum pay rates, particularly in areas where the demand for medical services is unmet. As healthcare facilities scramble to cover gaps in their rosters, the demand for locum doctors has increased, leading to higher pay rates for these positions. This trend is most pronounced in rural and remote areas, where locum GP pay rates can be up to 50% higher than in urban areas [AMA](https://ama.com.au).

The Australian Medical Association (AMA) has highlighted how the financial strain on general practices, due to stagnant Medicare rebates, is influencing locum pay rates. Practices are willing to pay a premium to ensure continuity of care, particularly in underserved regions. As a result, locum rates in these areas can vary significantly, with some positions offering lucrative compensation to attract doctors willing to work in challenging environments.
